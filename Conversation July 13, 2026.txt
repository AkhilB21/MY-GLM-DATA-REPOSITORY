2. Signal Hold / Debounce (prevents whipsaws at boundaries)

Once a signal fires, it stays active for N additional bars even if the condition fails. This prevents a signal from flickering on/off on consecutive days.

Example: A5 fires on Day 1 (4H RSI > 50). On Day 2, RSI drops to 49.5. With a 3-bar hold, A5 still fires on Days 2 and 3. Only if RSI stays below 50 for 4+ consecutive bars does the signal deactivate.
Most useful for: A5, A6, B1, B2, C2, C4 (the signals that hover near thresholds)
Hold period could be 2–3 bars for 4H signals, 3–5 bars for daily signals
3. Score EMA Smoothing (final layer)

Apply an exponential moving average to the final composite score (not individual signals). This dampens any remaining jumps from signals that aren't graded.

Formula: Smoothed Score = 0.6 × Today's Raw Score + 0.4 × Yesterday's Smoothed Score
Trade-off: introduces 1–2 day lag, but eliminates any residual single-day spikes
Could use a faster coefficient (0.7/0.3) to minimize lag
